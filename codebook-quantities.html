Physical Quantities Codebooks

物理量密码本

Physical quantities are the measurable aspects of the universe — velocity, displacement, time. These codebooks teach how to define them through the speed of light, and introduce the bridge from finite differences to calculus. 物理量是宇宙中可测量的方面——速度、位移、时间。 这些密码本通过光速来教授如何定义它们， 并引入从有限差分到微积分的桥梁。

Velocity Definition

速度定义密码本

Velocity as a Physical Quantity V = 0.41

Teaching Strategy: Velocity is introduced as a member of the class of physical quantities (γ), with light speed as the first named velocity. 教学策略：速度作为物理量(γ)类别的成员引入，光速是第一个命名的速度。

0.41 0.5 0.171 0.5 0.26 0.5 0.15 0.5 0.301

V ∈ γ = C — Velocity belongs to gamma (physical quantities), equals light speed 速度V 属于 γ（物理量），等于 光速C

This declares velocity (V, code 0.41) as a physical quantity belonging to the γ class (0.26). The first velocity we define is C — the speed of light — establishing it as the foundational reference velocity. 这声明了速度（V，编码0.41）是属于γ类（0.26）的物理量。 我们定义的第一个速度是C——光速——将其确立为基础参考速度。

Speed of Light Through Displacement and Time C = △X / △t

Teaching Strategy: Light speed is defined operationally as change in displacement divided by change in time, introducing the △ (delta) operator. 教学策略：光速被操作性地定义为位移变化量除以时间变化量，引入△（delta）运算符。

0.301 0.5 0.15 0.5 0.19 0.5 0.42 0.5 0.171 0.5 0.301 0.5 0.14 0.5 0.19 0.5 0.43 0.5 0.171 0.5 0.301

C = △X∈C ÷ △t∈C — Light speed equals change in displacement divided by change in time 光速C = 位移变化量÷时间变化量

This introduces the △ symbol (0.19, delta / finite difference) and defines velocity operationally. △X (0.19 0.42) means "change in displacement" and △t (0.19 0.43) means "change in time". The ∈C (0.171 0.301) qualifies that both quantities are measured in the context of light speed. Any civilization that measures motion will recognize: velocity = distance / time. 这引入了△符号（0.19，有限差分/变化量）并操作性地定义了速度。 △X（0.19 0.42）意为"位移变化量"，△t（0.19 0.43）意为"时间变化量"。 ∈C（0.171 0.301）限定这两个量都在光速的上下文中测量。 任何测量运动的文明都会认识到：速度 = 距离 / 时间。

From Finite to Infinitesimal △→0 : dX/dt

Teaching Strategy: The limit concept is introduced — when △ approaches 0, finite differences become differentials. This bridges algebra to calculus. 教学策略：引入极限概念——当△趋近于0时，有限差分变为微分。这是从代数到微积分的桥梁。

C = (△X∈C)→0 ÷ (△t∈C)→0 — When △ approaches 0, this becomes dX/dt C = 当△趋近于0时，变为 dX/dt（微分形式）

The → symbol (0.191) means "approaches" — the mathematical limit. Combined with 0, it reads "approaches zero". When △→0, the finite difference △X/△t becomes the derivative dX/dt — the instantaneous rate of change. This single expression teaches the fundamental concept of calculus: the limit transforms discrete change into continuous change. →符号（0.191）意为"趋近于"——数学极限。与0结合，读作"趋近于零"。 当△→0时，有限差分△X/△t变为导数dX/dt——瞬时变化率。 这个表达式教授了微积分的基本概念：极限将离散变化转化为连续变化。

Why Physical Quantities?

为什么需要物理量？

Units (0.3x) tell you what scale to measure with — Planck time, Planck length, speed of light. But physical quantities (0.4x) tell you what you are measuring — time, displacement, velocity.

This distinction is fundamental: a unit is a ruler, a quantity is what you measure with it. By separating the two, we can express any physical relationship with precision. The velocity definition also introduces calculus — the mathematics of change — showing that △X/△t and dX/dt are two views of the same truth. 单位（0.3x）告诉你用什么尺度来测量——普朗克时间、普朗克长度、光速。 而物理量（0.4x）告诉你你在测量什么——时间、位移、速度。

这个区别是根本性的：单位是尺子，物理量是你用尺子测量的对象。 通过将两者分开，我们可以精确地表达任何物理关系。 速度定义还引入了微积分——关于变化的数学—— 展示了△X/△t和dX/dt是同一真理的两种视角。
"Nature uses only the longest threads to weave her patterns, so that each small piece of her fabric reveals the organization of the entire tapestry."
— Richard Feynman

From the simple idea that velocity equals displacement over time, we derive calculus — the language of all change in the universe. A civilization that understands motion must understand limits. And a civilization that understands limits can understand everything we have to say.

从速度等于位移除以时间这个简单的概念出发， 我们推导出微积分——描述宇宙中一切变化的语言。 理解运动的文明必须理解极限。 而理解极限的文明能够理解我们要说的一切。
